Personal Income: Per Capita Income for U.S. Counties 2019

The map shows per capita income for counties in U.S.

  • Per capita income is the measure of the amount of money earned per person in a county. It can be interpreted as the average per-person income and is a useful indicator for the standard of living and quality of life of the population.
  • Areas with higher levels of income are shaded darker in the map.
  • Teton county in Wyoming has the highest average incomes per capita of any county in the United States, at $229,825. This is partly attributed to the high incomes of Jackson Hole residents, where property owners include Bill Gates [Source: Wikipedia].
  • Newyork (Newyork), Pitkin (Colorado) and Bristol Bay (Alaska) are the other highest-income counties.
